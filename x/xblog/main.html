We are Innovating


A significant advancement in autonomous robot ecosystem is a necessity towards any bigger dream. When it comes to building an ecosystem of robots then building a full scale robot does not always meet all the requirements. First of all it requires a significant amount of money to work on it, further they are less likely to survive in cases where certain parts of it or its dependent platforms fail. In such cases it would be a better decision to design and develop groups of robot / swarm of robots, each group with its own task and expertise. These groups { Network of robots } will communicate with other groups to carry out a complex job needing multiple levels of labour.

We are developing intelligent aerial-swarm robots with booster technology based on rocket engines. Each booster has its own brain and when they network together, the swarm will be able to complete complex tasks. We are currently designing and developing rovers and custom relandable model rocket engines. There is a lot to achieve when it comes to designing miniature sized rovers. Although a significant advances are made in rover technology with NASA producing a series of rovers to explore Mars environment like Curiosity, Spirit, Opportunity rovers but much advancements are not done in producing miniature size rovers such that a multiple number of rovers each with its own special objective can be send in a single launch. Of course producing miniature size rover with the objective of deploying it is a huge challenge in itself and will come with its own set of problems but on the upside it will also be economically much more efficient provided multiple rovers will be launched at a single go and further the area of exploration will increase by a large degree. Further failure of a single rover would not mean termination of the mission if multiple rovers of the same specifications can be sent. This can be achieved if the dimensions of the rovers are reduced considerably and the concept of swarm robots are put to use. The concept of modularisation in robotics can also serve a great amount into this idea.
